## Executive Summary
The paper introduces the Deep Tree of Research (DToR), a hierarchical framework for organizing and managing multiple research agents. A controller agent spawns and manages multiple "branches" of inquiry, each with its own research agent. This allows for a more comprehensive and in-depth analysis than a single agent, as it can explore multiple perspectives and resolve conflicts between them. The paper also introduces a local-first information retrieval strategy, where agents first consult a local knowledge base before turning to the web. This reduces the risk of hallucinations and improves the quality of the research.

## Idea Brainstorming
*What concepts are potentially useful for THIS project?*
- **Hierarchical Research:** The DToR framework can be directly applied to the existing "Debate Team" of agents. The `DeepTraderManager` can be adapted to act as the "DToR controller," spawning and managing the `BullResearcher` and `BearResearcher` as separate "branches" of inquiry.
- **Conflict Resolution:** The `DebateCoordinator`'s role can be expanded to synthesize the findings from these branches, resolving conflicts and producing a final, comprehensive report. This would improve the quality and reliability of the team's analysis.
- **Local-First RAG:** The project already uses a `KhalaMemoryToolkit`, which can be extended to serve as a local knowledge base. This would improve the quality of the research and reduce the risk of hallucinations.
